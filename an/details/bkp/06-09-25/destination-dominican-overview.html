Discover the Dominican Republic

The DR is a playground of peaks, rivers, and rugged beauty—perfect for travelers ready to dive deeper into the island experience.

Share
The Dominican people are known around the world for their warmth, energy, and open-hearted hospitality.

They’re some of the friendliest people you’ll ever meet—always quick with a smile, a laugh, or a helping hand. Their passion infuses every part of life, from the rapid-fire rhythm of their conversations to the vibrant colors of their clothing, the lively beats of merengue and bachata, and the joy they bring to every celebration.

Whether you’re exploring a mountain village or walking through a bustling city square, you’ll feel their welcoming spirit. In the Dominican Republic, strangers quickly become friends—and every visitor is treated like family.

Untamed & Unforgettable

The Dominican Republic is a stunning Caribbean paradise where breathtaking natural beauty meets heart-pounding adventure. From its pristine white-sand beaches and lush mountain landscapes to its vibrant culture and warm hospitality, the island offers something for every traveler.

Beyond the postcard-perfect scenery lies a world of exhilarating activities—ziplining through jungle canopies, horseback riding to hidden waterfalls, rafting down rushing rivers, or hiking dramatic peaks. Whether you crave relaxation or adrenaline, the Dominican Republic delivers an unforgettable blend of serenity and excitement in one unforgettable destination.

Dominican Republic

Dr quick facts

  • Location: Caribbean island of Hispaniola, shared with Haiti
  • Capital: Santo Domingo
    (the oldest European-founded city in the Americas)
  • Language: Spanish
  • Currency: Dominican Peso (DOP)
  • Climate: Tropical—warm and sunny year-round
  • Time Zone: Atlantic Standard Time (AST)
  • Highest Peak in the Caribbean: Pico Duarte (10,164 ft)
  • Longest River: Yaque del Norte—great for rafting and river adventures
  • Miles of Coastline: Over 900 miles, with countless beaches to explore
  • National Parks: 29 protected areas offering jungles, mountains, and marine life
  • Waterfalls: Over 100, including the famous Salto El Limón and Salto de Baiguate
  • Outdoor Activities: Hiking, canyoning, zip-lining, kiteboarding, whale watching
